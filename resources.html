A curriculum guide will accompany each of the six stories in Touching Home in China.

The guides will provide teachers and self-directed learners with classroom and homework activities related to each story, including thought-provoking discussion questions, textual analysis activities, interactive learning activities, hands-on projects, and research topics that strengthen students’ historical comprehension, analysis, and research capabilities. Links will take learners to relevant primary source materials, including news stories and journal articles curated by Touching Home in China.

The curriculum will meld content-rich material from Touching Home in China with expert pedagogy to maximize student engagement and relevancy to the lives of learners from middle school into college. Through the cross-cultural stories of American teen adoptees, who wrestle with their dual identity as daughters in Caucasian families, and only-child daughters growing up in rural China, secondary and post-secondary faculty will discover a wealth of prompts leading to probing questions. Inquiries will touch on a wide range of topics about China, including the changing lives of girls and young women.

Touching Home in China’s curriculum will help students learn to interrogate primary source documents, compare and contrast ideas, values, perspectives, and institutional behaviors, and examine the role of social and historical context in shaping the lives of individuals and the policy choices made by nations. Teachers and students interested in U.S. and China relations, Asian studies, human rights, human geography, gender studies, and social emotional development, especially involving issues of multiculturalism, abandonment, identity, belonging, and cultural curiosity, will discover a rich font of classroom resources in Touching Home in China.

The Henry Luce Foundation is providing generous support through a Special Project grant to enable us to develop the curriculum and initiate outreach to educators. Curriculum will be available in 2016.
